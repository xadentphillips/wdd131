Xaden's Simple Game Library Collection

In this age of crazy games and stimulus overload, it can be nice to get back to the simple root games of our childhood. Taking time to remember a simpler era and more fond memories can help you destress. Enjoy these quaint games and the fond memories that accompany them.

Memory Matching

  • Our earliest records of people playing memory matching games was in ancient China
  • The game was first known as "Sheng Ji" translated as "seeking the pair"
  • Concentration, as this game was nicknamed, became very popular during the Industrial Revolution
  • Concentration began to be produced by many game companies in the 1960s and 1970s with names like "Memory"

Tic Tac Toe

  • Games like Tic Tac Toe have been played since ancient Egypt
  • In England the game is known as "Noughts and Crosses"
  • Canadians call the game "Xs and Os"
  • Tic Tac Toe was first coded onto computers in 1952
  • In 1975 a computer was constructed made almost entirely out of Tinkertoys and was able to play Tic Tac Toe

Four in a Row

  • Allgedly, the first name for the game was "The Captain's Mistress" named so because a boat captain played it so often with one of his passengers
  • The game likely originated in the late 1800s or early 1900s
  • Instead of circles, the game was originally played with little balls
  • The game may have been inspired by tic tac toe as people tried to make it 3D
  • Unless they are stacked vertically, all winning four in a rows must go through the center column

Brick Breaker

  • Brick Breaker was developed for BlackBerry
  • Rather than a mouse or arrowkeys, the paddle was originally controlled by the BlackBerry trackpad
  • There are support groups for those with Brick Breaker addictions
  • Brick Breaker was created by a Canadian programmer
